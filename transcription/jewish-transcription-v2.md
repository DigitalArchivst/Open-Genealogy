## Hebrew Date Conversion
Hebrew year 5700 = 1939/1940 CE. To convert: Hebrew year − 3760 ≈ civil year (adjust for month). Rosh Hashanah falls September/October. Dates from Tishrei through December span two civil years (e.g., Tishrei 5652 = Sept/Oct 1891). Dates January through Elul: subtract 3760 for civil year. Leap years add Adar I; Adar II is the "real" Adar. Months: Tishrei, Cheshvan, Kislev, Tevet, Shevat, Adar (I & II in leap years), Nisan, Iyar, Sivan, Tammuz, Av, Elul.

## Common Hebrew/Yiddish Abbreviations
**Titles**: ר׳/רב=Rabbi; מו״ה=Moreinu HaRav; ב״ר=ben Rabbi; בן/בת=son/daughter of; אשת=wife of; אלמנת=widow of
**Memorial**: ז״ל=of blessed memory; ע״ה=peace upon him/her; נ״י=may his light shine (living); הי״ד=may God avenge (martyrs); נפ׳/נפטר=died
**Tombstone**: פ״נ/פ״ט=here lies buried; תנצב״ה=closing blessing
**Relationships**: אביו=his father; אמו=his mother; בנו=his son; בתו=his daughter; חתנו=son-in-law; כלתו=daughter-in-law
**Status/Occupation**: מלמד=teacher; שו״ב=ritual slaughterer; חזן=cantor; גבאי=synagogue official; אב״ד=head of rabbinical court; בחור=unmarried man; בתולה=unmarried woman; אלמן/אלמנה=widower/widow

## Document Type Reference
**Vital Records**: Birth—child's name, parents (names, ages), father's occupation, mother's maiden name, witnesses, mohel. Marriage—both parties, ages, parents, residence, witnesses. Death—deceased, age, parents or spouse, cause, burial.
**Revision Lists (Ревизские сказки)**: Family groups with ages; males listed first; notes on absences, deaths, military recruitment.
**Ketubah**: Aramaic formula with Hebrew; date, location, groom's name ben father, bride's name bat father, financial terms, witnesses.
**Matzevah**: Opening פ״נ/פ״ט; Name בן/בת Father's name; Hebrew death date; closing תנצב״ה; often includes age, origin, virtues, sometimes acrostic.
**Holocaust Documents**: German bureaucratic terms; Kennkarte (identity card), transport lists, ghetto registration. Handle with appropriate sensitivity.

## Sephardic/Mizrachi Records
Ladino (Judeo-Spanish) in Hebrew characters (Solitreo script), Latin, or Rashi script; spelling variable. Judeo-Arabic: Arabic in Hebrew characters. Naming uses "de" rather than "ben": Moshe de Avraham. Community terminology: kehila vs. kahal.
